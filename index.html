What to Wear

We love to dress up! Our wedding dress code is garden attire, which is basically fancy but on a lawn. High heels might not be as practical as a flat sandal or wedge. Remember no one ever regretted being over dressed.

Sonoma County is very hot in August, but temperatures drop substantially at night (really!). Since our wedding is almost entirely outdoors, you'll likely need a jacket or wrap once the sun goes down.

Travel & Transport

SFO: San Francisco International Airport

To get to Sonoma Country from SFO, rent a car or use the Sonoma County Airport Express. The drive takes you over the Golden Gate Bridge, but there can be substantial traffic during weekends. SFO is 1 hour and 20 minutes from Santa Rosa.

OAK: Oakland International Airport

To get to Sonoma County from OAK, rent a car or use Sonoma County Airport Express. OAK is about 1 hour and 10 minutes from Santa Rosa.

Wedding Transportation

St. Francis Winery has ample parking. We’ll also be providing a personalized UberEVENT code so you can get to the venue on us. Uber is reliable in the area, although may have long waits at night. Lyft is not as widely available.

What to Do

Gravenstein Apple Fair

The Gravenstein Apple Fair is on August 12th and 13th in Ragle Ranch Park in Sebastopol. There will be live music, crafts, local food, microbrews and wine, and a life on the farm area. Get ready for apple pie: Chris’s favorite dessert.

Cornerstone Sonoma

Cornerstone Sonoma is an outdoor marketplace with cute shops and amazing modern gardens. Chris loves the fruit trees here, and Emma loves the lavender. Cornerstone is one of our favorite spots in the area.

Sonoma Plaza

The City of Sonoma has an adorable town plaza that makes Emma feel like she truly lives in the west. Sonoma has great shops and restaurants, as well as a historic Mexican military outpost and Spanish mission village.

Calistoga

Calistoga is a luxe wine country town that’s home to the Sugar Train, a train car that is also a candy store!

Also

We recommend tubing on the Russian River, biking through Sonoma, grabbing a snack at SHED, and doing a wine tasting (or three).
