What to Wear

People dress up for weddings, and we like dressing up ourselves. Our wedding dress code is “garden attire,” which is basically fancy but on a lawn. High heels might not be as practical as a flat sandal or wedge. Remember no one ever regretted being over dressed.

Our wedding is almost entirely outdoors. Sonoma County is very hot in August, but temperatures drop substantially at night (really!). You’re likely to find yourself needing a jacket once the sun goes down.

Travel & Transport

SFO: San Francisco International Airport

To get to Sonoma Country from SFO, you can rent a car or use the Sonoma County Airport Express. The drive takes you over the Golden Gate Bridge, but there can be substantial traffic during weekends. SFO is about 1 hour and 20 minutes from Santa Rosa.

OAK: Oakland International Airport

To get to Sonoma County from OAK, you can rent a car or use Sonoma County Airport Express. OAK is about 1 hour and 10 minutes from Santa Rosa.

Wedding transportation

St. Francis Winery has a large parking lot, so you are welcome to drive to the wedding. We’ll also be providing a personalized UberEVENT code so you can get to the venue on us. Uber is reliable in the area, although it can be tricky late at night. Lyft is not as widely available. We may also provide a shuttle from the Flamingo hotel.

What to Do

Gravenstein Apple Fair

The Gravenstein Apple Fair will be held on August 12th and 13th in Ragle Ranch Park in Sebastopol. There will be live music, crafts, local food, microbrews & wine, and a “life on the farm” area. Get ready for apple pie (Chris’s favorite dessert).

Cornerstone Sonoma

Cornerstone Sonoma is an outdoor marketplace with cute shops & amazing modern gardens. Chris loves the fruit trees here, and Emma loves the lavender. Cornerstone is one of our favorite spots in the area.

Sonoma Plaza

The City of Sonoma has an adorable town plaza that makes Emma feel like she truly lives in the west. Sonoma has great shops & restaurants, as well as a historic Mexican military outpost & Spanish mission village.

Calistoga Hot Springs

Calistoga is a luxe wine country town that’s home to the Sugar Train, a train car that is also a candy store.. Calistoga is famous for its mineral hot springs--you can get a day pass at Calistoga Spa Hot Springs for $25.00.

We also recommend tubing on the Russian River, biking through Sonoma, grabbing a snack at SHED, and doing a wine tasting (or three).
